Historical Context

The Indonesian Rupiah was introduced in 1946, replacing the Dutch East Indies guilder. Since then, Indonesia has experienced periods of high inflation and currency depreciation, especially during the Asian Financial Crisis in 1997-1998 when inflation exceeded 58% and the rupiah fell from 2,600 to over 15,000 per USD.

Projection Methodology

Our projections beyond 2023 are based on historical trends, current economic policies, and global economic factors. We assume a gradual stabilization of inflation rates and currency depreciation over time as the Indonesian economy matures and strengthens.

Understanding Inflation & Exchange Rates

Inflation measures the rate at which prices rise, while exchange rates reflect the value of one currency against another. High inflation typically leads to currency depreciation as purchasing power decreases. The relationship between inflation and exchange rates is fundamental to understanding currency valuation.
